Project Overview

The NASA Lunabotics Challenge is an annual competition that challenges university teams to design, build, and operate autonomous lunar mining robots. Columbia University's participation represents our commitment to advancing space exploration technology through innovative mechanical design and autonomous systems integration.

As co-lead of the mechanical design team, I spearheaded early-stage research and prototyping efforts, focusing on wheel design optimization, material selection for lunar conditions, and collaborative integration with electronics and autonomy subsystems.

#MechanicalDesign #ProjectManagement #Prototyping #MaterialResearch

My Role

My Role as Co-Lead

  • Leadership in mechanical design efforts: Orchestrated the development of innovative wheel designs optimized for lunar regolith conditions, leading a team of 4 mechanical engineers through concept generation, analysis, and prototyping phases.
  • Collaboration with electronics and autonomy teams: Established cross-functional communication protocols and design interfaces to ensure seamless integration between mechanical systems and autonomous navigation capabilities.
  • Timeline and task management: Developed and maintained project schedules using Gantt charts and Agile methodologies, ensuring critical milestones were met while adapting to evolving technical requirements and resource constraints.
  • Material research and testing: Conducted comprehensive analysis of material properties for lunar environment compatibility, including temperature extremes, radiation exposure, and abrasive regolith interaction.

Design & Research Highlights

Initial Planning

Developed comprehensive project management plan with detailed Gantt charts tracking 6-month development timeline. Key milestones included concept validation, prototype testing, and competition preparation phases.

Status: Project plan completed and approved by faculty advisors

Wheel Design

Created two distinct wheel concepts: a flexible mesh design for optimal traction and a rigid cleated design for durability. Conducted FEA analysis on both designs to optimize for lunar regolith interaction.

Status: CAD models completed, material testing in progress

Material Research

Analyzed material properties for lunar environment compatibility. Compared aluminum alloys, titanium, and composite materials for structural components, considering temperature extremes and radiation exposure.

Status: Material selection matrix completed, final recommendations pending

Budgeting

Managed $15,000 budget allocation across mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems. Prioritized prototyping materials and testing equipment while maintaining contingency funds for unexpected challenges.

Status: Budget approved, procurement process initiated

Reflection & Next Steps

Project Progress & Learning

What We Achieved

Successfully completed initial design phase with two viable wheel concepts, established material selection criteria, and created comprehensive project management framework. Team collaboration protocols are working effectively.

Technical Challenges

Balancing wheel flexibility for traction versus structural integrity for durability proved more complex than anticipated. Material selection for lunar conditions requires extensive testing beyond standard engineering databases.

Key Learnings

Cross-functional communication is crucial for complex systems integration. Early prototyping revealed design assumptions that needed revision. Material properties in extreme environments require specialized research approaches.

Next Phase Goals

Complete material testing and finalize wheel design selection. Begin full-scale prototype construction and integration testing. Develop autonomous navigation algorithms in parallel with mechanical refinement.
